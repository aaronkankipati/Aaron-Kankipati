Neon Bull

I gave an AI ₹10,000 and full control to trade the Indian stock market.

A personal research experiment, not a product pitch. I wanted to know if Claude could autonomously make trading decisions on real NSE data and hold its own. No human approves any trade. It runs entirely on its own, twice a day, every market day.

The question I wanted to answer — Can AI autonomously grow money in a real stock market? Every decision in this build is oriented around answering that honestly, not proving it works.
How it runs — Two Cloudflare cron jobs fire every weekday, one at 9:30 AM IST and one at 1:30 PM IST. The Worker pulls live prices from Yahoo Finance NSE (free, no API key needed), feeds them to Claude Sonnet with RSI, SMA and volume data, and executes the trade. NSE holidays are auto-detected and skipped. No server anywhere. No manual trigger required.
Built with guardrails — Groww brokerage charges are simulated on every trade, including STT, DP charges and GST, so the P&L is realistic. The experiment auto-pauses if the portfolio drops below starting capital. Stock names are fully hidden from the public dashboard to stay clear of any SEBI compliance concerns.
Infrastructure — Built entirely on Cloudflare Pages, Workers and KV for storage. Running cost is zero. The live dashboard shows the portfolio, today's sessions, trade history and P&L in real time.

Moodfilm

I am not a developer by profession. I am a Product guy. I built this anyway.

An AI film curator that starts with a simple question: how are you feeling right now? Tell it your mood, pick a language (English, Hindi, or Telugu) and it finds a film matched to that emotional moment, not your watch history. Product thinking over code.

Discover the need — Most film apps recommend based on what you watched last week. I wanted something that started with how you feel right now. That gap felt worth solving.
Diagnose what was broken in v1 — I used my own product the way a real user would. Every poster showed the wrong film. The same blockbusters came up for every single mood. And there was no Hindi or Telugu support, which was the most requested thing after launch. Three clear problems, three backlog items.
Prioritise and ship v2 — Adding OMDb alongside TMDb pushed API calls to 170 per session against a free tier limit of 50. Rather than upgrade the plan, I redesigned the flow down to 22 calls, batched and parallelised. Claude now returns a numeric film ID so the poster is fetched directly. No guessing. Wrong poster eliminated. Language selection built in across every step.
The result — Loading takes 30 to 60 seconds, so I filled that time with 20 rotating film facts to keep users from dropping off. Every card now shows streaming availability by region so users know exactly where to watch. One tap sharing built in natively. 166 live sessions, 0 errors, 94% of issues resolved. Total cost: $0.
